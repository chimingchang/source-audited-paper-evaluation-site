Motivation

Scientific Taste Is Part Of The Research Problem

A useful scientific AI should not only summarize papers or predict citations. It should learn what makes a research direction deep, fertile, technically nontrivial, and robust under later evidence. That kind of taste is closely related to the ability to formulate good research questions: questions that are well-posed, connected to real obstacles, discriminating between alternatives, and likely to reveal structure rather than merely follow fashion.

Evaluate Contributions

Rank papers by source-audited scientific contribution, not by author authority, venue, citation count, or later fame.

Learn Taste

Use evaluation as a training signal for recognizing depth, originality, technical leverage, and field-shaping questions.

Ask Better Questions

Connect scores to predecessor gaps, unresolved claims, and claim-level fates so future questions target real scientific bottlenecks.

Score meaning

What The Scores Are For

The ranking is a measurement surface for a research-evaluation method. A score is meant to summarize a paper's evaluated contribution after source audit, predecessor comparison, claim-level scoring where needed, retrospective validation, uncertainty bands, and withheld calibration details.

Contribution signal

The score summarizes evaluated scientific contribution after predecessor audit, source completeness checks, and report-level evidence review.

Ex ante plus retrospective

Originality is preserved as a publication-time component, while later evidence can validate, leave unresolved, or cap claims.

Claim-aware structure

Different claims in one paper can have different later fates, so claim-level scoring is used when paper-level scoring would hide important structure.

Versioned artifact

Scores belong to a release. Evidence misses, predecessor corrections, or claim-fate updates should change future releases.

Interpretation guardrails

What This Site Is Not

Not an author ranking

Rows evaluate papers and score-bearing claims, not authors, groups, seniority, institutions, or authority.

Not a citation ranking

Citation count, public consensus, venue, and later fame are not substitutes for source-audited contribution.

Not a final verdict

A rank is a versioned evaluation under the current evidence set, uncertainty bands, claim fates, and calibration constraints.

Not hidden consensus

The public site withholds paper-specific calibration provenance; visible public scores must be interpreted through source evidence, score fields, and audit pages.

Public boundaries

How To Read The Public Site

The current public scope is hep-th / high-energy theory. The method is intentionally broader than that scope: source audit, predecessor controls, claim fates, uncertainty, and update discipline are meant to be portable to other research areas after new calibration sets are built.

  • Read rank clusters carefully. Adjacent rows with overlapping uncertainty bands should be treated as local clusters, not precise total order.
  • Inspect claims, not only paper scores. Some papers contain validated, unresolved, diagnostic, and invalidated claims at the same time.
  • Use audits to challenge scores. The best correction is specific evidence: missed predecessors, stronger follow-up evidence, no-go results, or claim-fate updates.
  • Respect withheld boundaries. Paper-specific calibration provenance is withheld in the public profile; public readers should evaluate the visible source evidence, report fields, and audit guardrails.

Feedback

What Feedback Is Most Useful

  • missed predecessors or earlier equivalent results;
  • follow-up evidence that validates, weakens, or invalidates a claim;
  • claim splits where one paper-level score hides multiple later fates;
  • calibration cases that expose authority, citation, recency, or hindsight bias;
  • methodology objections that can be turned into executable checks.

Update policy

How Public Changes Should Happen

1

Evidence first

Corrections should enter as source-audit, evidence-pack, claim-fate, or calibration updates before ranking changes.

2

Versioned outputs

Ranking YAML, generated pages, methodology, robustness, uncertainty, and readiness checks remain tied to versioned artifacts.

3

Public gate

The redacted public-site gate must pass before deployment, and the source repository remains separate from the site-only public repository.

4

Deployment record

Public deployments are pushed through the guarded deployment script and are traceable in the site-only repository history.
