Score construction

How The Public Score Is Read

Ex ante intrinsic score

Publication-time scientific contribution, judged against the closest predecessors. It excludes author prestige, citation count, journal status, and later fame.

Retrospective validity score

Later evidence: validation, uptake, counterexamples, no-go results, mature criticism, unresolved follow-up, or mature invalidation.

Maturity gate

Controls how strongly retrospective evidence may affect the final score: immature 0.3, active unresolved 0.5, mature validated 0.7, mature invalidated 0.8.

Validated final score

The public ranking score. It uses the credit-preserving base, then applies an invalidation cap when a mature-invalidated core claim requires one.

Credit-preserving base

credit_preserving_base_score =
  w * raw_originality_score
+ (1 - w) * ((1 - r) * ex_ante_non_originality_score
+ r * retrospective_validity_score)

w = 0.35. r is the effective retrospective weight after maturity gating. This preserves historical originality without letting failed core claims rank as validated science.

Caps

Invalidation Caps

Caps preserve historical credit in the report while preventing mature-invalidated central claims from being treated as validated science in the public ranking.

CategoryDefault capUse case
core_claim_invalidated55The central target claim or framework failed.

Claim-level scoring

One Paper, Multiple Fates

Claim-level scoring is used when a paper has separable score-bearing claims with different later outcomes. Structural claim weights are ex ante: centrality, load-bearing role, technical mass, and field-facing takeaway. Retrospective evidence changes claim scores, not the original weights.

Robustness

Rank Order Is Not Always Precise

Adjacent papers with overlapping uncertainty bands should be read as local score clusters unless a cross-case guardrail gives a stronger ordering. The compare page exposes small gaps, band overlap, maturity-mixed pairs, cap-bound rows, and claim-level sensitivity.

Withheld Signals Are Not Public Consensus

Paper-specific calibration provenance is not shown in this public profile. Withheld signals can trigger internal audits, but public ranking scores are read from source-audited score fields and do not directly depend on withheld calibration details.
